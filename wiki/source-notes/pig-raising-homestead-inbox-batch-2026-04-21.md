**Key claims** (public-safe; cite **raw** for wording):

- **Timber Creek** narrative: **rotational** paddocks reduce **mud/manure** buildup; **electric** perimeter plus **interior** divisions; **pig board** and **two-person** handling around **farrowing** sows; **boar** kept near some litters under observation (author reports **no** piglet aggression in their case—**not** a general rule).
- **Reddit thread**: repeated **homestead** advice to run **≥2** pigs for **social** stability; **electric** wire at **nose height** (and sometimes top) with **hog/cattle** panels; **rooting** under panels without **hot wire**; **solo gilt in heat** described as a **dangerous** failure mode (anecdote); **solar** energizers mentioned as compatible with **off-grid**.
- **Abundant Permaculture** article: **feeder pigs** ~**8 weeks**; **~80 ft² per pig** in a **static pen** vs more space / rotation for **odor** and **muscle**; **castration** framed around **boar taint** concerns; **apple cider vinegar** in water in **first week** as a **folk** gut-health step (**not** validated here).

**Open questions / follow-ups**

- Ingest **USDA NRCS** / **land-grant extension** swine or **pastured pork** primers for **TN** (or target state) to **upgrade** authority on **space**, **manure**, and **health** programs.
- **Zoning** and **on-farm slaughter / custom exempt** rules are **jurisdiction-specific**—keep out of wiki **unless** sourced from **official** PDFs already in `raw/`.
