# Crown and cross-slope — Penn State Center for Dirt & Gravel Road Studies (PDF)

**Raw PDF**: [`raw/processed/2026/tb-crown-and-cross-slope.pdf`](../../raw/processed/2026/tb-crown-and-cross-slope.pdf)  
**Machine extract**: [`raw/processed/2026/tb-crown-and-cross-slope-extracted.md`](../../raw/processed/2026/tb-crown-and-cross-slope-extracted.md)

**Summary**: **2019** technical bulletin—three surface templates: **center crown**, **in-slope** (common on **steep sidehills**; notes **superelevation** on curves), **out-slope** (sheet flow to fill side; can eliminate ditches on low-volume roads). Explains purpose of **thin sheet flow** vs concentrated flow. **Maintenance**: traffic and grading deform cross-section; **fines loss** → **W-shaped** profile and ponding; **unpaved** roads need **roughly 2–3×** paved cross-slope—recommends **4–6%** (**½–¾″ per foot** width) vs ~2% typical for pavement.
