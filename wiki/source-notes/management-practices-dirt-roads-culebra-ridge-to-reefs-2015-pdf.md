# Management practices for dirt roads — Ridge to Reefs / Culebra (PDF)

**Raw PDF**: [`raw/processed/2026/management-practices-for-dirt-roads-final-fy13.pdf`](../../raw/processed/2026/management-practices-for-dirt-roads-final-fy13.pdf)  
**Machine extract**: [`raw/processed/2026/management-practices-for-dirt-roads-final-fy13-extracted.md`](../../raw/processed/2026/management-practices-for-dirt-roads-final-fy13-extracted.md)

**Summary**: **August 2015** guidance (Culebra, P.R. context) on **dirt road construction, maintenance, and retrofitting** to reduce **sediment** to nearshore waters. States bare roads and soils can transport sediment at **10–1000×** natural hillslope rates (cites Ramos-Scharrón and MacDonald, 2007). Organized into **diversion practices**, **flow/erosion reduction**, **example grading/ditching scenarios**, and **general maintenance**—explicitly **not** a full engineering manual; points to FAO and other references for heavier design.
