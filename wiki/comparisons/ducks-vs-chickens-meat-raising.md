## Comparison table

| Dimension | Meat ducks (esp. Pekin / heavy breeds) | Meat chickens (Cornish-type broilers) |
|-----------|----------------------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| **Typical grow-out** | Often discussed around **7–12+ weeks** depending on breed and pinfeather cleanup ([Bramblewood](../../raw/processed/2026/raising-ducks-for-meat-bramblewood-hill.md)); one homesteader processed **broilers ~7 wk** vs ducks ~1 wk later in a side-by-side batch ([Reddit import](../../raw/processed/2026/ducks-for-meat-reddit-homestead.md)) | **~7–9 weeks** common for **Cornish Cross**-style birds in hobby literature ([`raising-chickens-for-meat`](../source-notes/raising-chickens-for-meat.md)); **pastured** setups in imports reference **tractors** and **pasture rotation** |
| **Housing / moveable systems** | **Tall, mobile, fenced runs** often preferred over low **tractors**—ducks are **more mobile** and **messy with water** ([Reddit import](../../raw/processed/2026/ducks-for-meat-reddit-homestead.md)) | **Chicken tractors** (Salatin-style) and **deep litter** / **pasture** systems are common patterns in imports ([`deep-litter-chicken-tractor-pasture-systems`](../../raw/processed/2026/deep-litter-chicken-tractor-pasture-systems.md), [`chicken-tractor-plans-permies-forum`](../../raw/processed/2026/chicken-tractor-plans-permies-forum.md)) |
| **Water** | **High labor**: spillage, bathing, drinking behavior can mean **many gallons/day** in hot weather per anecdote ([Reddit import](../../raw/processed/2026/ducks-for-meat-reddit-homestead.md)); **ponds** can be **damaged** by duck use unless designed for it | **Standard drinkers**; lower water drama than ducks in most accounts |
| **Feed efficiency / economics** | One anecdote: ducks may **eat more** yet **dress smaller** than broilers in a same-season compare—**not measured scientifically** in source ([Reddit import](../../raw/processed/2026/ducks-for-meat-reddit-homestead.md)) | Broilers often framed as **predictable** meat in small-farm guides ([`raising-chickens-for-meat`](../source-notes/raising-chickens-for-meat.md)) |
| **Processing — pluck** | **Pinfeathers** and **down** make plucking **slow** without specialized gear; **wax** or strong **pluckers** may change the calculus ([Reddit import](../../raw/processed/2026/ducks-for-meat-reddit-homestead.md)) | **Plucking** generally faster with common **drill pluckers** per same thread; extensive **chicken** butchering how-tos in imports |
| **Meat quality / use** | **Dark**, **rich** meat; **fat** layer useful for **confit**/roasting ([Bramblewood](../../raw/processed/2026/raising-ducks-for-meat-bramblewood-hill.md)); breast often cooked **medium-rare** in enthusiast accounts ([Reddit import](../../raw/processed/2026/ducks-for-meat-reddit-homestead.md)) | **White meat** dominant; **Cornish** carcass familiar to most consumers |
| **Breeding / repeatability** | Many **heritage/heavy** ducks can be **bred on-farm**; **hybrid Pekin** lines exist from hatcheries ([Bramblewood](../../raw/processed/2026/raising-ducks-for-meat-bramblewood-hill.md)) | **Cornish Cross** usually **purchased** each batch; **slow-growth** and **dual-purpose** alternatives discussed elsewhere in vault |
| **Noise / neighbors** | Ducks can be **loud** ([Reddit import](../../raw/processed/2026/ducks-for-meat-reddit-homestead.md)) | Roosters often restricted; **meat-only** flocks can avoid crowing if **all-female** policy works for your plan |
| **Harvest psychology** | Same **kill** skills as chickens; forum content on **first-time mental barriers** applies to any poultry ([`butchering-chickens-first-time-mental-permies`](../source-notes/butchering-chickens-first-time-mental-permies.md)) | Multiple **butchering** walkthroughs ([`how-to-butcher-a-chicken-abundant-permaculture`](../source-notes/how-to-butcher-a-chicken-abundant-permaculture.md), Prairie Homestead import) |

## Verdict (heuristic)

- Choose **meat chickens** (especially **Cornish-type**) when you want **faster**, more **predictable** batches, **easier plucking**, and **tractor/pasture** systems that are **well documented** for chickens.
- Choose **meat ducks** when you value **dark meat**, **duck fat**, and **on-farm breeding** options for **heavy breeds**, and you can budget **water handling**, **fencing**, and **plucking time**—start **small** the first year per the Reddit anecdote ([`ducks-for-meat-reddit-homestead`](../../raw/processed/2026/ducks-for-meat-reddit-homestead.md)).

**Confidence: medium** — mix of **one** vivid side-by-side anecdote, **blog** guidance, and **forum** threads; seek **extension** or **enterprise budgets** for economics.
