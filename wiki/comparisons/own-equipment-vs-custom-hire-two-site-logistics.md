# Own equipment vs custom hire under two-site logistics

## Question

With **two sites** and **non-trivial commute**, when should **owned** equipment live at **`SITE_FARM`**, at **`SITE_HOMESTEAD`**, or be **hired/custom**—vs duplicating tools?

## Criteria

| Criterion | Own at farm | Own at homestead | Custom hire |
|-----------|-------------|------------------|-------------|
| **Utilization** | High **acres**-hours on farm | Home projects + **transport** to farm | Pay per **acre/job** |
| **Time tax** | No **trailer** move each trip | Must **haul** or accept **duplicate** | Vendor **scheduling** risk |
| **Capital** | **CAPEX** + **shelter** + **theft** risk | Same, smaller | **OPEX** only |
| **Best when** | Core enterprise **requires** timeliness (hay, fence, spray window) | **Maintenance** of home systems; **shared** truck | Low **annual** hours or **specialist** skill (laser leveling, large pour) |

## Tradeoff summary

- **Distance** increases **hauling cost**—either **cache** equipment at the **work site** or **hire** locally.
- **Telemetry** does not remove **metal**; it changes **when** you discover you need the tool.
