# Agritourism business plan — guest hub on 120 acres, family home 35 min away

**Prompt**: Outline a **realistic business plan** for a **small, family-owned agritourism** operation with **~120 acres** as the **working farm and guest hub**, and the **proprietors’ residence on ~5 acres** **~35 minutes away by car**. The plan should be candid about **which animals belong where** and how **distance from home** shapes operations.

**Disclaimer**: This is a **planning narrative**, not legal, tax, insurance, zoning, food-safety, or labor advice. Agritourism sits at the intersection of **land use**, **short-term lodging**, **events**, and sometimes **on-farm food**—rules are **jurisdiction-specific**. Align operations with licensed professionals and local authorities. Related vault syntheses: [`Agritourism smart hobby farm — tiny houses and guest work`](../analyses/agritourism-smart-hobby-farm-tiny-houses-guest-work-stays.md), [`Agritourism, tiny houses, and Tennessee hobby farms`](../analyses/agritourism-tiny-houses-tennessee-hobby-farm.md), [`Farm stocking — 120 acres vs 5 acres (research prompt)`](../analyses/farm-stocking-120-acres-vs-5-acres-research-prompt.md).

---

## 1. Executive summary

**Concept**: A **family brand** that sells **overnight farm stays**, **day experiences**, and **seasonal events** **on the 120 acre parcel**—where **pasture, livestock, equipment, and guest lodging** coexist. The **~5 acre parcel** is the **private family home** (and optional **personal** garden or hobby animals), **not** the agritourism venue.

**The 35 minute drive** is now the **owners’ commute**: between **home** and **the farm business**. That creates a **coverage problem**—guests, livestock, and emergencies need **someone on or near the farm** far more often than “drive out twice a day” allows. The plan must budget for **on-farm presence** (family rotation, **resident caretaker/innkeeper**, or **seasonal** booking windows), not assume a suburban commute model.

**Realistic positioning**: **“Stay on a working farm”** is coherent—**tours, chores, and production** happen in one place. Profitability still leans on **lodging ADR**, **events**, and **labor discipline**; commodity livestock alone rarely carries the business.

---

## 2. Site roles (locked before animal choices)

| Site | Primary role | Why |
|------|----------------|-----|
| **~120 acres** | **Working farm + guest hub**: lodging, parking, welcome, **all agritourism animal encounters**, pasture rotation, hay, bulk poultry batches, handling pens, shop/CSA pickup, equipment | Guests expect the **farm experience** where the **animals and land** are; **setback and zoning** for **dwellings** must be resolved here (not on the 5 acre lot unless regulations allow a second residence—often a different permit path). |
| **~5 acres** | **Private residence** for proprietors: **off-duty**, **admin**, **school bus**, **mailing address**, optional **personal** garden or small non-commercial flock—**not** the public face of the business unless you deliberately brand a second location | Keeps **family life** and **guest operations** separable for **privacy** and **liability** clarity; reduces **neighbor** friction from STR traffic at home. |

**Operating rule**: **Commercial livestock and guest-facing animals live at the 120** (with **buffer fencing** and **clear guest routes** so people are not wandering through working pens). **Do not** duplicate a second production herd at the 5 acre home for the **same enterprise**—it doubles **feed, vet, and time** for no guest benefit. If the 5 acre parcel holds animals, treat them as **personal/hobby** with **strict** separation from **farm biosecurity** (boots/vehicle hygiene when driving farm→home).

## 3. Animals — what goes where (realistic)

### 3.1 One-hundred-twenty acres — all business animals (production + guest story)

**Guest-adjacent “soft” stock** (for tours, eggs, controlled contact):

- **Laying hens** near (but not inside) guest parking—coop/run designed for **odor**, **predators**, and **guest pathways**; hand-washing station nearby.
- **Small goats or sheep** in a **dedicated petting/education paddock** with **stout fence**, **escape plan**, and **supervision rules**—not mixed loose with **guardian dogs** or **working cattle** unless professionally laid out.
- **Bees** sited with **flight paths** away from decks and **signage**.

**Production stock** (choose **one primary** line to start, expand slowly):

- **Beef** (cow-calf or stocker) on **rotational grazing**—handling pen, water, hay plan per [`Farm stocking — 120 acres vs 5 acres (research prompt)`](../analyses/farm-stocking-120-acres-vs-5-acres-research-prompt.md) methodology.
- **Sheep or goats (meat)**—predator control and **fencing** are non-optional.
- **Pastured poultry (meat birds)** in **batches**—processing logistics as before (haul-out vs on-farm rules).

**Layout discipline**: Separate **“pretty” guest sightlines** (orchard edge, small-animal yard) from **working alleyways** (cattle squeeze, loader, manure spreader). **Insurance** and **visitor policies** must reflect **real** machinery and **bull / ram** risk—not Instagram defaults.

### 3.2 Five acres — home only (optional personal use)

- **Optional**: **Family garden**, **few laying hens for household eggs**, **pets**—scaled so **evenings at home** stay restorative, not a second farm job.
- **Avoid**: Running **parallel commercial** flocks or herds here unless a family member is **dedicated** to that site—it **competes** with sleep and **blurs** liability between “home” and “business.”

### 3.3 Why the old “split herd across two farms” problem shrinks—but a new problem appears

- **Biosecurity** improves when **commercial animals** are **one place**.
- **New constraint**: **Who is on the 120** when a guest **locks themselves out**, a **water line** breaks, a **calf** is down, or a **predator** hits the chicken tractor at **10 p.m.**? A **35 minute** owner drive is **not** a welfare or hospitality answer. The business plan must name **coverage**: **on-farm housing** for a rotating family member, **paid caretaker**, **reduced hours** (no check-ins after dark without staff), or **strong automation + local emergency contacts** (still not a full substitute).

## 4. Product and revenue (realistic mix)

**Core offers** (examples—pick 2–4):

1. **Lodging on the 120** (cabins, tiny homes, farmhouse rooms, glamping)—**highest margin** if occupancy works; **cleaning** and **turnover** must be scheduled with **commuting** owners in mind (batch turns, hired cleaner **local** to the farm).
2. **Farm tours and chore experiences**—**same site** as lodging; no shuttle narrative required.
3. **Seasonal events**—lambing weekends, harvest dinners, markets—**parking and egress** planned for **rural road** capacity.
4. **Workshops**—fiber, poultry demos (where legal), **bee talks**.
5. **Farm store / CSA pickup** at the **farm gate** on the **120**.

**Avoid early overreach**: **Weddings** and **commercial kitchens** still multiply permits—stage later.

---

## 5. Operations and the 35-minute commute (owners)

- **Coverage matrix**: Document **who sleeps on-farm** on **guest nights**; if **nobody**, you need a **contracted innkeeper** or **caretaker** within **short minutes** of the property.
- **Batch owner tasks**: **Fence, hay, vet** visits on **set days**; **admin and procurement** can happen from the **5 acre** home office.
- **Tools and inventory**: Keep **vet kit**, **backup generators**, and **guest lockout spare** **on the 120**; avoid “only at home” dependencies for emergencies.
- **Remote monitoring**: Cameras on **lodging approach**, **water tanks**, **freezer**, **gate**—reduces panic drives; see [`Smart agriculture, Meshtastic, and LoRaWAN`](../topics/smart-agriculture-meshtastic-and-lorawan.md) for **field IoT** context—not a substitute for **human response** when guests are on site.

---

## 6. Team and seasonality

- **Roles**: **Innkeeper / guest messaging** (can be same person as **livestock lead** on small operations—dangerous without backup); **bookkeeping** from home; **weekend** family rotation onto the **120** during peak season.
- **Seasonality**: If **off-season** means **no guests**, owners may still **commute** for **livestock**—model **fuel and time** explicitly; consider **reducing** herd in early years to match **labor**.

---

## 7. Marketing and brand

- **Single story**: “**Stay on our working farm**”—honest photos of **real** mud, **real** fences, and **real** chore times (within safety marketing).
- **Privacy**: Market the **5 acre home** as **not** part of the guest tour unless you **choose** a rare “dinner at the owners’” upsell with clear boundaries.

---

## 8. Financial snapshot (illustrative)

- **Insurance**: **Farm + agritourism + lodging** where **machinery**, **animals**, and **overnight guests** **co-locate**—often **stricter** than a suburban STR; shop **specialists**.
- **Labor**: Budget **caretaker** or **cleaning** **local to the 120** to limit **owner miles**.
- **Two properties**: **Property tax**, **utilities**, and **maintenance** on **both** parcels—**5 ac** is not revenue-generating in this model unless you add a **separate** approved use.

**Breakeven**: Lodging and events still often **subsidize** livestock; **don’t** assume **drive time** is free.

---

## 9. Risks (short list)

- **Emergency response**: Owner **35 minutes away** when a **guest** or **animal** crisis hits—mitigate with **SOPs**, **local contacts**, **on-farm housing** in season.
- **Security**: **Empty guest units** between bookings; **equipment theft**; **livestock** and **public** on same parcel—**lighting**, **locks**, **insurance**.
- **Zoning**: **Dwelling** for owners **on the 120** (even a **modular** manager home) may be the **practical** alternative to perpetual commuting—verify **accessory dwelling** and **farm labor housing** rules.
- **Burnout**: **Commute + hospitality + calving** is a **three-job** stack—**hire or simplify**.
