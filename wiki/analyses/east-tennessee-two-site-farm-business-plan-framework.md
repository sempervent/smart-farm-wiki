# East Tennessee two-site farm business plan — planning framework

**Package navigation (reading order)**: [`East Tennessee two-site farm business plan (package)`](../business-plan/east-tennessee-two-site-farm-business-plan.md)

## Purpose of this framework

This wiki holds a **decision-grade**, **phased** business plan for a future operation with:

| Fact | Value |
|------|--------|
| Homestead / ops base | ~**5 acres**, East Tennessee — **not** primary production |
| Production holding | ~**120 acres**, East Tennessee — **enterprise mix undecided** |
| Separation | ~**35 minutes** one-way |
| Intent | **Side business with profit intent** → possible **salary replacement** |
| Horizon | **10 years** |
| Labor | **Family** (father / son / grandson primary) |
| Capital | **Low initial**; **moderate phased** investment; **selective** financing of infrastructure |
| Operating philosophy | **Heavy telemetry / automation** (treated as **capital + OPEX + failure modes**, not magic) |
| Optimization rank | **1.** Profit → **2.** Low labor → **3.** Sustainability / resilience |

The **framework** (this page) defines **what pages exist**, **in what order to build them**, **how candidate 120-acre enterprises will be compared without premature commitment**, and **what is known vs assumed vs unknown**. Later edits **fill** the child pages with numbers, scenarios, and decisions.

**Non-goals**: Single-enterprise prescription before validation; lifestyle branding; optimism without maintenance and degraded-mode costs.

## Order of creation (why)

1. **Vision + constraints** — Without stop rules and capital ceiling, enterprise scoring is meaningless.
2. **Two-site operating context** — Travel time and “where the brain lives” drive OPEX, batching, and automation placement before enterprise choice.
3. **Enterprise options** — Full scenario matrix; compares **mixes**.
4. **Recommended strategy** — Commits to **strategy family** (not a single commodity); branching logic.
5. **Labor / family** — Weekly rhythm, feasibility filter.

7. **Capital + financing** — Infrastructure **sequence** + debt cautions.

## Decision framework — comparing candidate enterprise paths (120 acres)

Use this **before** treating any single enterprise as the plan.

### Step A — Generate candidates

Explicit **strategies** (not single commodities), e.g.:

- **Grazing-led** (stocking + forage math)
- **Row-crop / commodity** (equipment + scale + weather)
- **Specialty crop** (labor spike + marketing)
- **Agritourism / events** (lodging or day-use; regulatory surface)
- **Mixed rotation** (complexity vs resilience)
- **Timber / long-rotation** (cash timing vs liquidity)

Each candidate must state **primary revenue**, **secondary revenue**, and **what stays off the 120** (homestead role clear).

### Step B — Feasibility gates (kill / defer)

| Gate | Question |
|------|----------|
| **Land/water** | Soil, slope, water rights, fencing, infrastructure vs enterprise needs |
| **Labor budget** | Fits family hours + seasonal peaks (informed by labor page) |
| **Capital budget** | Fits phased CAPEX; financing only where justified |
| **Distance / batching** | 35-min remote: compatible with checks, harvest, vet, equipment? |
| **Regulatory** | TN county/zoning, food, agritourism, livestock movement |
| **Exit** | Can strategy be unwound without stranded asset? |

### Step C — Score (rubric below)

Normalize scores **1–5** per dimension (higher = better **for that row’s “good direction”**; see rubric notes). Compute **tier composites**, then **weighted objective** (profit emphasis).

### Step D — Sensitivity

On **top 2–3** drivers (usually price, yield, labor hours, capital), run **±20%** scenarios; mark **fragile** vs **robust** strategies.

### Step E — Phasing and optionality

Split actions into:

- **Reversible / cheap** — preserve multiple enterprise paths
- **Irreversible / expensive** — require explicit gate and date

Document **pivot triggers** (metrics + dates) in roadmap and validation backlog.

## Weighted evaluation rubric

### Objective hierarchy (explicit weights)

| Tier | Objective | Weight in final score |
|------|-----------|----------------------:|
| 1 | **Profit** (risk-adjusted operating margin / acre, path to salary replacement) | **0.45** |
| 2 | **Low labor** (owner hours + family peak load, net of automation relief) | **0.35** |
| 3 | **Sustainability / resilience** (financial + operational shock absorption) | **0.20** |

**Composite** (0–5 scale recommended for each tier):

`Score = 0.45 × Tier1 + 0.35 × Tier2 + 0.20 × Tier3`

Each **tier** is built from the dimension groups below (average or weighted average inside tier — document choice per candidate).

### Dimension table (candidate enterprise strategy × score)

Score each dimension **1–5**. Direction: **5 = best for a commercially serious, automation-leaning family operation** unless noted.

| Dimension | What it measures | Notes for scoring |
|-----------|------------------|-------------------|
| **Expected profitability** | Long-run margin potential (after full-cost allocation) | Include **owner labor at replacement wage** in costs |
| **Labor intensity** | Human hours per $ revenue (or per acre) | **Invert for Tier 2**: high labor hours → **low** score |
| **Automation / telemetry fit** | Where sensing and control reduce trips, false negatives, and variance | Penalize “automation theater” (high cost, low leverage) |
| **Capital intensity** | Up-front $ and financed $ per unit revenue | **Invert** if high capital blocks phased plan |
| **Time to first material revenue** | Months/years to positive operating cash (enterprise-specific) | Faster **can** score higher unless it trades away profit |
| **Resilience — market/weather** | Margin stability under price and yield shocks | Diversification, storage, contract channels |
| **Fit — East Tennessee** | Climate, markets, infrastructure, extension/ecosystem | Use [`East Tennessee — profitable crops matrix`](east-tennessee-profitable-crops-matrix.md) as input, not oracle |
| **Fit — 35-minute remote site** | Batchability, need for “eyes on,” theft/vandalism, emergency response | Penalize strategies needing **constant presence** without staff |
| **Family operability** | Skill match, succession clarity, single-point-of-failure risk | |
| **Regulatory / liability complexity** | Licensing, food safety, agritourism, transport, insurance | **Invert**: more complexity → lower score unless priced in |

### Mapping dimensions → tiers (default)

| Tier | Primary dimensions (average or weighted) |
|------|------------------------------------------|
| **Tier 1 — Profit** | Expected profitability (50%), capital efficiency (inverse capital intensity) (25%), time-to-revenue reasonableness (25%) — adjust weights in child page if needed |
| **Tier 2 — Low labor** | Inverted labor intensity (40%), automation fit (35%), family operability (25%) |
| **Tier 3 — Resilience** | Market/weather resilience (50%), ET fit (25%), inverted regulatory burden (25%) |

**Remote-site fit** (`Fit — 35-minute remote site`): apply as **multiplier** (0.85–1.00) on Tier 2 **or** fold into Tier 2 average — pick one method and hold constant across candidates.

### Decision rule (skeleton)

- **Shortlist** candidates with composite **≥ threshold T** (set after first pass with real numbers).
- **Disqualify** any candidate failing a **hard gate** (legal, water, uninsurable exposure).
- **Prefer** candidates that stay **viable** under pessimistic Tier-1 sensitivity before optimistic scaling.

## What must be decided before major irreversible spend

| Decision | Before… |
|----------|---------|
| **Primary vs secondary revenue** architecture for the 120 | Large fence, pond, barn, irrigation, perennials |
| **Livestock vs crop vs hospitality** regulatory path | Insurance and licensing spend |
| **Where CAPEX lives** (homestead vs remote) for telemetry and power | Duplicate vs single-site brain |
| **Financing vs pay-as-you-go** for equipment | Interest + cash flow stress |
| **Minimum viable monitoring** vs **nice-to-have** sensors | OPEX and alert fatigue |
