# Business viability and farm economics — gap analysis (strategic audit)

**Purpose**: Evaluate what the repository **still lacks** for **business viability** and **farm operations economics**, using [`Strategic audit — decision-safe operations for a two-site smart farm`](strategic-audit-decision-safe-operations.md) and [`Implementation backlog — strategic audit (P0–P3)`](implementation-backlog-strategic-audit.md) as authorities. **Not** legal, tax, or investment advice—**gaps and page targets** only.

**Scope**: Revenue, **enterprise selection**, **market channels**, **staffing**, **insurance**, **accounting**, **CAPEX/OPEX**, **logistics**, and **risk**. Technology architecture gaps are **out of scope** here except where they drive **economic** choices (e.g., telemetry ROI, fixed-infrastructure justification).

**What already helps (brief)**

- [`East Tennessee — profitable crops matrix`](east-tennessee-profitable-crops-matrix.md) — enterprise **signals** and links to **UT budgets** and CPA; still **not** your farm’s numbers.
- [`Tennessee hobby farm and small-farm business sources`](../topics/tennessee-hobby-farm-and-small-farm-business-sources.md) — **pointers** and excerpts; not an integrated **operating model**.
- [`Agritourism business plan — guest hub on 120 acres, family home 35 min away`](agritourism-dual-site-business-plan-five-and-120-acres.md) — **one** worked **lodging-led** dual-site narrative; **bias risk** if production is not agritourism-first.
- [`Dual-site operations model — non-agritourism farm business`](dual-site-operations-model-non-agritourism.md) — **first draft** logistics framing; **no** revenue model inside.

## 3. Decisions you cannot responsibly make *from this repository alone*

These remain **under-modeled** until **your numbers**, **professional advice**, or **ingested primary sources** land in the vault (audit **§7–8** echoes this).

1. **Whether a specific enterprise “pays for”** a barn, cooler, irrigation, or telemetry mast on **120 acres**—without **unit economics** and **cash-flow** timing.
2. **Optimal revenue mix** (commodity vs direct vs agritourism) for **your** skills, risk tolerance, and **drive time**.
3. **Full cost of split-site logistics** (fuel, depreciation, spoilage, **extra trips**) vs **consolidating** assets on one parcel.
4. **Insurance adequacy** and **entity structure** (LLC, umbrella, agritourism riders)—the wiki can hold **questions**, not **coverage limits**.
5. **Hire vs contract** classification and **workers’ comp** requirements in **Tennessee**—needs **state** rules + professionals.
6. **Market access**: whether **price and volume** assumptions in UT budgets are **achievable** for your **location and marketing**.
7. **Livestock carrying capacity** and **purchase timing**—prompt-level only until **forage math** and **local markets** are grounded.
8. **Whether automation reduces labor enough** to pay for itself—**no ROI** model tied to **actual** labor hours and wage assumptions.
9. **Tax treatment** of equipment, two homes, personal vs farm use—**not** documented.
10. **Debt service** capacity and **lender covenants**—not in wiki.
