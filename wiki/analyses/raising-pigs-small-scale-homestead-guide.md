## 1. Decide the track (feeder vs breeding)

| Track | When it tends to fit | Notes from captures |
|-------|----------------------|---------------------|
| **Two feeder pigs** | Meat for the freezer; limited land/time | Abundant Permaculture frames **spring** purchase, grow **4–5 months**, avoid **winter** carry costs ([`raising-pigs-for-beginners-abundant-permaculture-inbox-2026-04-21.md`](../../raw/processed/2026/raising-pigs-for-beginners-abundant-permaculture-inbox-2026-04-21.md)). Reddit: **two** pigs ≈ same labor as one ([`general-pig-raising-advice-reddit-homestead-inbox-2026-04-21.md`](../../raw/processed/2026/general-pig-raising-advice-reddit-homestead-inbox-2026-04-21.md)). |
| **Breeding herd** | Land for **rotation**, tolerance for **farrowing** risk | Timber Creek describes **sows**, **boar**, **litters**, **maternity** paddocks ([`how-to-raise-pigs-naturally-timber-creek-farmer-inbox-2026-04-21.md`](../../raw/processed/2026/how-to-raise-pigs-naturally-timber-creek-farmer-inbox-2026-04-21.md)). Reddit warns **AI / boar** management is **“next level”** vs feeders. |

## 2. Gates before animals

- **Zoning**: Abundant Permaculture reminds readers to check **city** rules if inside limits ([`raising-pigs-for-beginners-abundant-permaculture-inbox-2026-04-21.md`](../../raw/processed/2026/raising-pigs-for-beginners-abundant-permaculture-inbox-2026-04-21.md)).
- **Transport**: Plan **crate**, **bedding**, and **stops** on long hauls (same source). **Biosecurity** and **branding/ID** requirements are **state-specific**—not in these captures.
- **Neighbor / road risk**: Timber Creek cites **roads** and **neighbors** as drivers for **strong** perimeter control ([`how-to-raise-pigs-naturally-timber-creek-farmer-inbox-2026-04-21.md`](../../raw/processed/2026/how-to-raise-pigs-naturally-timber-creek-farmer-inbox-2026-04-21.md)).

## 3. Space, rotation, and rooting

- **Static pen sizing (heuristic only)**: One capture suggests **~8×10 ft** per pig as a **minimum** “happy if they can sleep, eat, root” framing—**double** for two ([`raising-pigs-for-beginners-abundant-permaculture-inbox-2026-04-21.md`](../../raw/processed/2026/raising-pigs-for-beginners-abundant-permaculture-inbox-2026-04-21.md)). Treat as **starting literature**, not a legal stocking rate.
- **Rotation**: Timber Creek rotates so **vegetation** can recover and **manure/mud** does not accumulate on one pad ([`how-to-raise-pigs-naturally-timber-creek-farmer-inbox-2026-04-21.md`](../../raw/processed/2026/how-to-raise-pigs-naturally-timber-creek-farmer-inbox-2026-04-21.md)). Reddit stresses **rooting** will rework **pasture** and **woodlot** unless **moved** ([`general-pig-raising-advice-reddit-homestead-inbox-2026-04-21.md`](../../raw/processed/2026/general-pig-raising-advice-reddit-homestead-inbox-2026-04-21.md)).
- **Woodland / brush**: Thread anecdote: pigs use **trees** for fodder/shade but **small stems** may need **caging** until tall enough ([`general-pig-raising-advice-reddit-homestead-inbox-2026-04-21.md`](../../raw/processed/2026/general-pig-raising-advice-reddit-homestead-inbox-2026-04-21.md)).

## 4. Fencing and power

Consensus across captures: pigs **test** fences—**electric** training is a recurring theme.

- **Panels + hot wire**: Reddit: **hog panels** too short without **hot** wire; **cattle panels** slightly taller; **root** under panels if **no** electric ([`general-pig-raising-advice-reddit-homestead-inbox-2026-04-21.md`](../../raw/processed/2026/general-pig-raising-advice-reddit-homestead-inbox-2026-04-21.md)). Timber Creek uses **electric** with **wood/pallet** interior divisions ([`how-to-raise-pigs-naturally-timber-creek-farmer-inbox-2026-04-21.md`](../../raw/processed/2026/how-to-raise-pigs-naturally-timber-creek-farmer-inbox-2026-04-21.md)).
- **Off-grid**: Thread mentions **solar** fence energizers as a workable pattern ([`general-pig-raising-advice-reddit-homestead-inbox-2026-04-21.md`](../../raw/processed/2026/general-pig-raising-advice-reddit-homestead-inbox-2026-04-21.md)).
- **Energizer sizing**: One comment cites **“3 joules minimum”** for **poly** wire setups—**vendor/engineering** sources should replace this for your design ([`general-pig-raising-advice-reddit-homestead-inbox-2026-04-21.md`](../../raw/processed/2026/general-pig-raising-advice-reddit-homestead-inbox-2026-04-21.md)).

## 5. Shelter, water, feed

- **Shelter**: Wind/sun/rain breaks; **deep bedding** (straw, sawdust, or **wood chips** per sources); pigs **rub**—secure corners and posts ([`how-to-raise-pigs-naturally-timber-creek-farmer-inbox-2026-04-21.md`](../../raw/processed/2026/how-to-raise-pigs-naturally-timber-creek-farmer-inbox-2026-04-21.md); [`general-pig-raising-advice-reddit-homestead-inbox-2026-04-21.md`](../../raw/processed/2026/general-pig-raising-advice-reddit-homestead-inbox-2026-04-21.md)).
- **Water**: Large **barrel** drinkers appear in both Abundant Permaculture and Reddit examples ([`raising-pigs-for-beginners-abundant-permaculture-inbox-2026-04-21.md`](../../raw/processed/2026/raising-pigs-for-beginners-abundant-permaculture-inbox-2026-04-21.md)).
- **Feed**: Expect **grain supplement** even on pasture in these narratives; **scraps/forage** as extras ([`how-to-raise-pigs-naturally-timber-creek-farmer-inbox-2026-04-21.md`](../../raw/processed/2026/how-to-raise-pigs-naturally-timber-creek-farmer-inbox-2026-04-21.md)). **Organic** premix mentioned as a **cost** tradeoff in Abundant Permaculture article.

## 6. Safety and temperament

- **Sow + piglets**: Timber Creek: **never** assume pet-like behavior—**pig board**, **two people** when handling piglets near dam ([`how-to-raise-pigs-naturally-timber-creek-farmer-inbox-2026-04-21.md`](../../raw/processed/2026/how-to-raise-pigs-naturally-timber-creek-farmer-inbox-2026-04-21.md)).
- **Boar + piglets**: Same source experimented with **boar** adjacent to some litters; treat as **observed anecdote**, not a **safe default** ([`how-to-raise-pigs-naturally-timber-creek-farmer-inbox-2026-04-21.md`](../../raw/processed/2026/how-to-raise-pigs-naturally-timber-creek-farmer-inbox-2026-04-21.md)).
- **Heat / isolation**: Reddit story about a **solo gilt** in **estrus**—**stress + escape** risk; use as a prompt to plan **companionship** and **handling** protocols ([`general-pig-raising-advice-reddit-homestead-inbox-2026-04-21.md`](../../raw/processed/2026/general-pig-raising-advice-reddit-homestead-inbox-2026-04-21.md)).

## 7. Health, parasites, and castration (defer to professionals)

- **Castration**: Abundant Permaculture links **on-farm** castration and cites **boar taint** as a reason some producers castrate ([`raising-pigs-for-beginners-abundant-permaculture-inbox-2026-04-21.md`](../../raw/processed/2026/raising-pigs-for-beginners-abundant-permaculture-inbox-2026-04-21.md)). **Animal welfare** and **skill** requirements vary—use a **vet** or qualified tech if unsure.
- **Parasites / medicated feed**: Reddit thread mentions **medicated deworming feed** and cutting off **~30 days** before harvest ([`general-pig-raising-advice-reddit-homestead-inbox-2026-04-21.md`](../../raw/processed/2026/general-pig-raising-advice-reddit-homestead-inbox-2026-04-21.md)). **Withdrawal intervals** are **label- and jurisdiction-specific**—this wiki does **not** set a number for you.
- **Apple cider vinegar**: Listed in Abundant Permaculture as a **first-week water additive** ([`raising-pigs-for-beginners-abundant-permaculture-inbox-2026-04-21.md`](../../raw/processed/2026/raising-pigs-for-beginners-abundant-permaculture-inbox-2026-04-21.md)); evidence strength **not** evaluated here.
