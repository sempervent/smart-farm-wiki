# American ginseng from seed — ASAP start plan (Appalachian hardwood context)

**Routing**: Operator sequence for starting **American ginseng** (*Panax quinquefolius*) **from seed** on **East Tennessee–style** hardwood forest sites. **Provenance hub**: [`American ginseng — seed and forest culture sources (inbox batch 2026-04-19)`](../source-notes/american-ginseng-seed-forest-culture-sources-inbox-batch-2026-04-19.md) (now includes **Tennessee TDEC** program material, **MTSU/TSU/UT** wild-simulated guide, and **UT Crops** ginseng soil notes). **Not** legal, tax, or medical advice.

**Four-part suite** (same scenario, deeper operations): [`Site evaluation + materials checklist`](american-ginseng-site-evaluation-and-materials-checklist.md) · [`Planting and cultivation guide`](american-ginseng-planting-and-cultivation-guide.md) · [`Woodlot maintenance, pathology, and remote security`](american-ginseng-woodlot-maintenance-remote-security-and-trail-cameras.md) · **this page** (ASAP timeline + **TDEC** gates).

**Mandatory vs optional**

| Tier | Scope |
|------|--------|
| **Mandatory** | Read **current** **Tennessee Department of Environment & Conservation (TDEC)** Natural Areas **ginseng program** materials for **harvest**, **dealer**, and **export** obligations before you **dig**, **buy**, or **ship** roots across state lines. Captured summaries below are **secondary** to the live program site. |
| **Mandatory for seed success** | **Refrigerate** stratified seed until planting; **fall** sowing per extension + vendor cluster; **do not** let purchased seed **dry**. |
| **Optional / pilot** | Small **test plot**; **deer** exclusion on high-browse sites; follow **MTSU-region** wild-simulated PDF for **float testing** and **seed sanitation** steps if you adopt that protocol (**bleach is hazardous**—use the **PDF’s exact times/rinses**, PPE, and disposal practice). |

## Tennessee operational gates (from ingested captures)

**Agency**: The **Tennessee Ginseng Program** is administered under **TDEC** (Natural Areas), not county Extension offices. Use the program hub linked from [`tn-tdec-ginseng-program-page-capture-inbox-2026-04-19.md`](../../raw/processed/2026/tn-tdec-ginseng-program-page-capture-inbox-2026-04-19.md) as your **bookmark**.

**Wild harvest on private land** (as summarized on the captured **TDEC** page and consistent with the **2018** Tennessee Ginseng Program slide deck [`tn-ginseng-regulations-andrea-2018-capture-inbox-2026-04-19.pdf`](../../raw/processed/2026/tn-ginseng-regulations-andrea-2018-capture-inbox-2026-04-19.pdf)):

| Rule (captured wording) | Implication for planning |
|-------------------------|---------------------------|
| Collection window **September 1–December 31** (inclusive) for wild ginseng on **private lands** with **landowner permission** | Any **dig-for-sale** or **personal** wild harvest you intend to be **lawful** in Tennessee must fall inside this calendar band—**not** during spring seeding work. |
| Plants must have **three or more prongs** and **mature red berries**; **replant berries** after harvest | If you ever harvest from your own woods, plan **berry replanting** as part of the **same** site visit. |
| **No Tennessee permit** to dig for personal use or sale is described on the captured TDEC page; **dealer permit** required for dealers **purchasing** Tennessee-collected ginseng for **resale** or **export** | Starting **seedlings only** does **not** replace **dealer** obligations if you later **buy** or **broker** roots. The **2018** program deck extract also cites a **\$250/year** dealer registration fee and a **September 1 → August 31** permit year—**re-verify** current numbers on **TDEC**. |
| **Buying season** for dealers: from **September 1** (**green** roots) and **September 15** (**dry** roots) through **March 31** (inclusive) per TDEC page capture | Shapes when **legal commerce** can occur even if horticulture continues year-round. |

**Export / interstate movement** (2018 program deck extract): moving ginseng **out of Tennessee** for commercial purposes is described as requiring a **dealer permit** plus **export certificate** workflow—treat this as a **flag to verify** with **TDEC** and **U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service** instructions, not a DIY checklist here.

**Certified dealers**: the **2025-09-15** list PDF in `raw/processed/2026/` is the **captured** dealer roster (**do not** republish directory rows in wiki pages—open the **PDF** locally).

**TN conservation status**: **Tennessee Smart Yards** capture lists the species as **“Special Concern, Commercially Exploited”** statewide—frame plantings as **stewardship**, not extraction from public land (Wilson County MG capture repeats **CITES** stewardship messaging).

## What “ASAP” actually means for ginseng seed

**Biology**: Ripe seeds typically require a **long dormancy cycle** (roughly **18–20 months** / **double dormancy** in Wikipedia + VCE extracts). Wild-simulated **direct planting** of **fresh** berries means you **wait** for natural stratification in soil.

**Purchased stratified seed**: Suppliers pre-condition dormancy. **Tennessee wild-simulated guide** extract recommends **refrigerated** storage until planting and a **July–August** purchase window for seed (confirm with your vendor’s region). Vendor sheet (**Richters** capture) still emphasizes **plant ASAP** after receipt; **dominant** forest planting remains **fall**.

**If you are reading this in spring (e.g. April)**: **(1)** Complete **TN gate** review above; **(2)** **Site proof** + soil test; **(3)** **Order** stratified seed for **late-summer arrival** if using the **July–August** sourcing pattern from the MTSU-region guide; **(4)** **Plant October–November** where possible (VCE + vendor cluster); **(5)** If you already hold stratified seed in spring, keep it **cold/moist** per vendor guidance—**do not** improvise warm storage.

## Phase 1 — Same season (site proof, soil, seed order)

**Site + soil** (combine **VCE** + **MTSU-region** extract):

- **Shade** (~**75–90%**); **well-drained** slopes; avoid **wet feet**.
- **pH** targets cited in MTSU-region extract around **5–6** for healthy plants; **calcium** and **phosphorus** sufficiency is discussed with **East Tennessee** soil-study citations in that PDF—**run a soil test** and map results to those chapters rather than guessing amendments.
- **Indicator plants**: MTSU-region guide lists **range-overlap** companion species; use as **field clues** + compare to **Tennessee Smart Yards** profile for **statewide** habitat wording.

**UT Crops ginseng note** ([`ut-crops-ginseng-soil-nutrient-capture-inbox-2026-04-19.md`](../../raw/processed/2026/ut-crops-ginseng-soil-nutrient-capture-inbox-2026-04-19.md)): captured chart indicates **no additional nitrogen sidedress** for establishment—avoid **heavy manure** / aggressive **N** that pushes **disease** in other extension narratives.

## Phase 2 — Fall planting window (core “start”)

**Seed QC** (merge **VCE** + **MTSU-region** extract):

- Buy **stratified** seed from **reputable** sources; MTSU-region text warns about **mis-labeled** stratification—use **co-ops / known dealers** where possible.
- **Float test** (sink vs float) and **pre-plant sanitation** appear in the MTSU-region PDF—if you use them, **follow the PDF exactly** (concentration, time, rinse). **Household bleach** is **toxic** and can **damage seed** if mishandled.

**Planting pattern**: keep **United Plant Savers** wild-simulated broadcast template from the cluster ([`american-ginseng-cultivation-growing-guide-third-party-capture-inbox-2026-04-19.md`](../../raw/processed/2026/american-ginseng-cultivation-growing-guide-third-party-capture-inbox-2026-04-19.md)) **or** the bed methods from **VCE**—do **not** mix density doctrines without documenting which you used.

**Security**: maintain **private** maps; public pins increase **theft** risk (popular sources + extension tone).

## Phase 3 — Year 1 after sowing (establishment)

| Task | Notes |
|------|------|
| **Spring scout** | Watch **damping-off** in cool wet springs |
| **Moisture** | Mulch / leaf litter—avoid **waterlogging** |
| **Pathogens** | Alternaria cycles tied to leaf litter in handbook material—remove infected litter where feasible |
| **Deer / slugs** | Repeated browse resets stages; slug pressure on first-year seedlings |

## Realistic horizons (planning)

- **Emergence**: stratified seed → **next spring** after fall sow (vendor logic).
- **Seed-bearing maturity**: commonly **multi-year** (**5–10**-year class in VCE population discussion).
- **Marketable root**: **multi-year to decade** horizon depending on intensity; **prices** in older PDFs are **not** current—use **certified dealer** PDF + live markets when selling.

## Tennessee / two-site deployment note

On **Campbell / Anderson**-style parcels, treat ginseng as **optional**, **high-stewardship**, **long-horizon** **woodland enterprise**: it **competes** for attention with **deer pressure**, **security**, and **TDEC** commerce rules. It does **not** replace **forage / beef** cash-flow spines in the East Tennessee package.
