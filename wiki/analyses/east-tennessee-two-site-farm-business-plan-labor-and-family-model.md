# Labor model and weekly operating rhythm

## Purpose

Make **family capacity** and **time** explicit for a **two-site** operation: **~5 ac** home base (planning, records, shop, **telemetry ops**) and **~120 ac** production (**field labor**, **livestock**, **equipment**), separated by **~35 minutes** one way (planning default—**measure**). The weekly template is a **starting hypothesis**; **surge weeks** (calving, hay, shipping) **override** it.

**Coverage detail**: [`Weekly coverage matrix — two-site farm operations`](weekly-coverage-matrix-two-site-farm-operations.md), [`Family labor model and coverage matrix — two-site smart farm`](family-labor-model-and-coverage-matrix-two-site-smart-farm.md).

## Labor philosophy

- **Family-first** labor pool (father / son / grandson as **named** roles in your copy).
- **No phantom hired hand**: if help is needed, **budget** and **insure** it in **Phase 3+**.
- **Travel-time-aware**: batch **field** work; **do not** assume **daily** presence at **120** without **resident** labor or **hired** coverage.

## Roles (assign real names locally)

| Role | Typical duties | Peak risk |
|------|----------------|-----------|
| Ops lead | Schedule, sales coordination, vet, major equipment moves | Burnout in surge |
| Field lead | Rotations, water, fence, handling | Injury; fatigue |
| Tech lead | Broker, sensors, backups, spares | False confidence in dashboards |

## Weekly rhythm (template — grazing-led)

| DOW | Homestead (~5 ac) | Production (~120 ac) |
|-----|-------------------|----------------------|
| Mon | Plan week; order parts/materials | — or short check if alert |
| Tue | Shop / fab / print | **Batch visit**: rotate, water, eyes on stock |
| Wed | Books / records / compliance | — |
| Thu | Telemetry review; firmware/patch window | **Batch visit** |
| Fri | Family admin | — or preflight weekend work |
| Sat | Optional deep work | Heavy tasks if needed |
| Sun | Rest / exception | Calving or hay **override** |

**Rule of thumb (Phase 2+ grazing)**: minimum **two** batched **120** visits per week—increase during **surge** ([`Recommended enterprise strategy`](east-tennessee-two-site-farm-business-plan-recommended-enterprise-strategy.md)).

### Why the weekly template is usually wrong (until proven)

The DOW grid is a **hypothesis**, not a promise. **Execution illusion**: the farm will follow the grid because it reads sensibly on paper.

**Falsifier**: one **measured** surge week (calving, hay weather, processing squeeze, major escape) where **logged hours** exceed the template’s implied capacity. Until **V11** includes **at least one** such window and the **coverage matrix** shows **who absorbs overload**, treat the grid as **unproven**—especially under the **35-minute** split ([`distance-tax rules`](two-site-operations-model-5ac-homebase-120ac-production.md)).

## Seasonal surge (placeholders)

| Window | Surge type | Mitigation |
|--------|------------|------------|
| Calving (if cow-calf) | Possible **daily** need | Temporary stay near **120**, reduce herd complexity, or **hire** |
| Hay | Weather windows compress work | Custom hire or partner—avoid **solo** equipment trap |
| Weaning / shipping | Trailer and handling hours | Pre-book buyer or auction slot |

## Telemetry and labor

| Telemetry outcome | Labor effect |
|--------------------|--------------|
| Tank OK | Skip unnecessary trip |
| Tank bad | Dispatch—batched or emergency |
| False alarm | **Costs** labor in triage—**budget** time in weekly plan |

## Phased labor expectations

| Phase | Expectation |
|-------|-------------|
| 0 | Low hours; paperwork and maps |
| 1 | Field days **2–4×/month** + surveys |
| 2 | Often **0.3–0.5 FTE combined family** on farm work—**validate** |
| 3 | Scale or **explicit** hired fraction |
| 4 | Possible full-time equivalent on-farm **if** margin supports |
