# Tennessee farm business entity options — LLC, sole proprietorship, partnership

## Requirement vs recommendation vs professional gate

| Category | What this page does |
|----------|---------------------|
| **Official requirement** | **None** stated here—requirements are **statute- and fact-specific** (cite `raw/` captures and TN/IRS pages). |
| **Operational recommendation** | For **profit intent**, **two counties**, and **future scaling**, **start** documentation habits (separate accounts per UT guidance) **even before** entity choice is final. |
| **Professional gate** | **Attorney**: equity splits, land-lease layering, agritourism, processing. **CPA**: tax classification, Schedule F vs entity returns, sales tax. |

## Two-site pattern (Claxton vs Demory)

| Topic | `SITE_HOME` (~5 ac) | `SITE_FARM` (~120 ac) |
|-------|---------------------|------------------------|
| **Zoning / nuisance** | Often **stricter** municipal or ETJ rules | County rules + **access/road** realities |
| **Property tax classification** | May or may not qualify for **greenbelt** buckets depending on **use + acreage** | Likely candidate for **agricultural** classification **if** statutory tests met—**assessor** decides |
| **Operating entity** | May hold **admin / payroll / invoicing** even if production is remote | **Production revenue** and **inventory** often attributed here—**map in books** with CPA |

## Mode changes (what to re-check)

- **Agritourism / events** → commercial liability + possibly **different** business license posture (see insurance checklist + county).
- **Employees** → workers’ comp + payroll tax + posters/registrations (baseline page).
- **Leased land** → contract layer may **override** default liability assumptions—**attorney**.
