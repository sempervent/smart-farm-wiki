# East Tennessee — profitable crops matrix (planning synthesis)

**Question**: Which **crops and crop-like enterprises** are among the **most profitable** options for an **East Tennessee** farm?

**Epistemic note**: “Profitable” is **not** the same as “largest statewide cash receipts.” This page separates **revenue scale** (commodity agriculture) from **margin per acre and labor** (direct-market horticulture) and points to **UT Extension enterprise budgets** and **Center for Profitable Agriculture (CPA)** materials so you can plug in **your** yields, prices, and costs. East Tennessee spans **elevation and microclimate** variation; a regional gardening note in this vault places much of East TN in roughly **USDA zones 6b–7a** ([`Tennessee garden beginners (Willow Ridge)`](../source-notes/tennessee-garden-beginners-willow-ridge.md))—treat as orientation, not a site-specific forecast.

## How to read the matrix

- **Profit signal** summarizes *why* an enterprise often appears on “high potential” lists—not a guarantee.
- **Fit (East TN)** is agronomic/climatic plausibility at a high level.
- **Main risks** are recurring ones; see UT budget disclaimers for year-to-year price and input volatility.
- **Typical channels**: where producers often capture value (commodity elevator vs direct retail, etc.).
- **Planning tools**: authoritative UT (and related) resources to quantify returns.

## Matrix — enterprise clusters

| Enterprise cluster | Profit signal (why it shows up) | Fit (East TN) | Main risks | Typical channels | Planning tools |
|--------------------|--------------------------------|---------------|------------|------------------|----------------|
| **Row crops** — corn, soybeans, winter wheat, grain sorghum; often **wheat–soy double-crop** | High **total farm revenue** potential at **scale**; equipment amortized over many acres | Common in TN cropping rotations; East TN participation varies by county/soils/topography | Thin **per-acre** margin; weather, herbicide resistance, fertilizer/fuel price swings | Elevators, contracts; **2026** field-crop budgets emphasize **short-run** planning | [UT AREC field crop budgets (2026)](https://arec.tennessee.edu/extension/budgets/) — PDF/Excel; [Profitable Marketing Decision Aid (2026)](https://arec.tennessee.edu/wp-content/uploads/sites/17/2026/02/ProfitableMarketingDecisionAid-2026.xlsm) |
| **Cotton** | Strong where adapted; irrigated budgets available | **West and Central TN** more typical than mountain East TN; verify local history and gins | High management; pest/water constraints | Gin/contract system | Same [field crop budgets](https://arec.tennessee.edu/extension/budgets/) — cotton sheets |
| **Hay / pasture / forage** | Steady demand for **livestock** region; can complement cattle | Cool-season and warm-season forages both used; fescue common | Weather, fertility, equipment; price vs quality | On-farm use, local horse/cattle market, retail small bales | [Forage budgets & calculator](https://arec.tennessee.edu/extension/budgets/) — hay, pasture, baleage PDFs linked there |
| **Burley / dark tobacco** | Historical **high value per acre** where quota/contract systems align | Still budgeted for **TN–KY** region in UT materials; **contract- and regulation-heavy** | Labor, compliance, single-buyer dynamics, health/regulatory scrutiny | Contract buyers | [Tobacco budgets](https://arec.tennessee.edu/extension/budgets/) — burley, dark air-cured, dark fired |
| **Tomatoes (commercial / small-scale)** | High **price per lb** with strong direct and wholesale windows | Outdoor + tunnel common in TN; disease pressure in humid summers | Labor, **disease** (sample budgets stress planning), market timing | Farmers markets, CSAs, wholesale | [Sample small-scale tomato budget (2022)](https://utia.tennessee.edu/publications/wp-content/uploads/sites/269/2023/10/W1125.pdf); [vegetable budget hub](https://vegetables.tennessee.edu/grower-resources/crop-budgets-and-prices/) |
| **Bell peppers (large-scale)** | Good retail/wholesale **unit value** | Warm season; East TN feasible with IPM | **Phytophthora blight** and other disease scenarios explicitly modeled in UT sample | Wholesale, retail | [Bell pepper sample budget (2022)](https://utia.tennessee.edu/publications/wp-content/uploads/sites/269/2023/10/W831.pdf) |
| **Blueberries** | Multi-year **U-pick + retail** models; premium pricing | **Chill-hour** cultivar choice matters; suited to many East TN sites | Frost, birds, **IPM cost**, establishment lag | U-pick, ready-picked, agritourism adjacency | [Blueberry IPM × marketing budget (2020)](https://utia.tennessee.edu/publications/wp-content/uploads/sites/269/2023/10/D102.pdf) |
| **Blackberries** | Strong per-pound value; primocane types extend season | Widely grown in upper South | Disease (fungicide programs in budgets), labor at harvest | U-pick, retail | [Blackberry budget (2022)](https://utia.tennessee.edu/publications/wp-content/uploads/sites/269/2023/10/D154.pdf) |
| **Cut flowers — summer annuals** | Very high **revenue per acre** potential when marketing works | Heat/humidity—cultivar and succession matter; CPA highlights **Greene County** grower story | Labor, perishability, wedding/event demand swings | Farmers markets, florists, events | [Summer annual cut flower budgets (TN)](https://utia.tennessee.edu/publications/wp-content/uploads/sites/269/2025/10/W1348.pdf); CPA project (below) |
| **CPA “landscape” crops** — *cut flowers, persimmons, blueberries, elderberries, garlic, microgreens* | UT CPA narrowed ~50 crops to **six** for statewide interviews and outreach—signals **diversification** interest, not a profitability ranking | East TN can support all six with site-specific planning (drainage, slope, infrastructure) | **Marketing** and **labor** dominate; microgreens = food safety + quick turns | Direct sales, restaurants, co-pack, on-farm | [Tennessee Farm Bureau — CPA specialty crop project](https://tnfarmbureau.org/ut-extension-center-for-profitable-agriculture-project-focuses-on-specialty-crop-production); [CPA](https://cpa.tennessee.edu) |
| **Industrial hemp (CBD floral)** | Was a **speculative** high-value crop; UT published **1-acre CBD biomass** budgets | Agronomically possible; **market collapsed** from early peaks | Licensing, THC compliance, extraction contracts, price risk | Contract processors | [2020 hemp CBD budget (PDF)](https://utia.tennessee.edu/publications/wp-content/uploads/sites/269/2023/10/D41.pdf) — treat as **historical planning**; re-validate markets |

## Statewide context (does not pick East TN winners)

USDA NASS **Tennessee** summaries show **soybeans, corn, hay, cotton, wheat, tobacco** among major **value** categories in statewide statistics—useful for understanding **commodity weight**, not for predicting your farm’s margin. See [USDA NASS Quick Stats — Tennessee overview](https://www.nass.usda.gov/Quick_Stats/Ag_Overview/stateOverview.php?state=TENNESSEE).
