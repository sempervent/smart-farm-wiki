## 1. Pick a production doctrine

| System | Sketch (from TDEC + extension captures) |
|--------|-------------------------------------------|
| **Wild-simulated** | Minimal soil movement; broadcast or drill under leaf litter; densities closer to natural patches; slower labor, lower input |
| **Woods-grown** | Prepared beds under natural canopy; may allow **fertilizer / pesticide** toolkit where legal—higher labor + disease vigilance |
| **Artificial shade** | Outside this guide’s core—see VCE materials if you pivot |

**Hankins VCE 354-312** [`vce-pub-354-312-ginseng-forest-farming-hankins-capture-inbox-2026-04-20.pdf`](../../raw/processed/2026/vce-pub-354-312-ginseng-forest-farming-hankins-capture-inbox-2026-04-20.pdf) centers **wild-simulated** as a perpetual, self-seeding model if harvest is conservative. **VCE 354-313** PDF from the 2026-04-19 cluster adds **ecology**, **diseases**, and **profit** tables—read both before designing beds.

## 2. Seed handling (common across sources)

1. **Buy stratified** seed from reputable dealers (warned in Beyfuss test PDF and TN wild-simulated guide extracts).
2. **Store refrigerated** and **never fully dry** between receipt and planting.
3. Optional **float test** + **surface sterilization** sequence appears in [`ginseng-testing-potential-woodland-sites-capture-inbox-2026-04-20-extracted.md`](../../raw/processed/2026/ginseng-testing-potential-woodland-sites-capture-inbox-2026-04-20-extracted.md)—if used, **copy timings verbatim** from the PDF; rinse thoroughly; do not leave bleach residues.

## 3. When to plant

- **Northeast protocol** in the Beyfuss testing PDF: **late September–late October** as the most common successful window.
- **Tennessee + vendor cluster**: **October–November** remains the practical target; align with **weather** and **supplier** ship dates.

---

## 4. Bed preparation and sowing patterns

| Method | Steps (high level) |
|--------|---------------------|
| **Beyfuss 3′ test plot** | Clear **3 ft square**, scratch mineral soil, broadcast **50** stratified seeds, replace leaf litter (testing PDF). |
| **UPS / wild-simulated strip** | Rake **beds** across slope, broadcast **5–7 seeds/ft²**, rake downhill litter to cover (2026-04-19 cluster excerpt—see source-note). |
| **VCE 354-313** row / bed methods | See forestlands bulletin for alternate densities and disease spacing guidance. |

**Planter hardware**: [`ginseng-planter-comparisons-capture-inbox-2026-04-20.pdf`](../../raw/processed/2026/ginseng-planter-comparisons-capture-inbox-2026-04-20.pdf) is **image-first** (empty text extract)—open the **PDF** if comparing mechanical planters vs hand methods.

## 5. Cultivation practices (years 0–5)

- **Weed & brush control**: keep air moving—**Alternaria** risk rises with stagnant, humid canopies (354-313 + pathology deck cross-reference).
- **Nutrition**: UT Crops capture [`ut-crops-ginseng-soil-nutrient-capture-inbox-2026-04-19.md`](../../raw/processed/2026/ut-crops-ginseng-soil-nutrient-capture-inbox-2026-04-19.md) shows **no nitrogen sidedress** for the pictured establishment guidance—avoid “push growth” fertility unless a **soil test** + specialist plan justifies it.
- **Calcium / phosphorus**: align amendments with **MTSU-region** wild-simulated PDF (2026-04-19 ingest) after lab results—do not guess from wiki tables.
- **Water**: natural leaf-litter mulch usually suffices; irrigate only where drought stress is observed and legal water rights exist.

## 6. Record-keeping for Tennessee compliance

Even when **planting**, log **seed source**, **planting date**, **plot GPS (private)**, and **photos** of canopy—later **harvest** and **dealer** interactions under **TDEC** require disciplined provenance.
