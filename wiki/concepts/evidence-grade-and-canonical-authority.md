# Evidence grade and canonical authority

**Purpose**: Give readers a **small vocabulary** for **how much to trust** a page and **where to start** so the vault stays navigable as analyses accumulate.

## Canonical pages (durable scope)

**Canonical** pages **own** a topic for the **East Tennessee two-site** package or a **named** system (e.g. telemetry SoR). They are **updated in place**; new pages **extend** them rather than **competing** with parallel essays. Anchors: [`East Tennessee two-site farm business plan (package)`](../business-plan/east-tennessee-two-site-farm-business-plan.md), [`Structural audit — page ownership, entity gaps, and hub routing`](../analyses/structural-audit-page-ownership-entity-gaps-and-hub-routing.md).

## Supporting analyses

**Supporting** analyses provide **matrices**, **artifacts**, **runbooks**, or **time-boxed** validation detail. They **do not** redefine the strategy owned by the canonical chapter— they **link back** to it.

## Exploratory / anecdotal

**Exploratory** material (blogs, forums, listicles) may inform **ideas** or **failure-mode color**; it is **not** a substitute for **extension**, **agency**, or **primary legal** sources for **operational, financial, regulatory, or safety** claims. Mark **low authority** in prose or `confidence: low` where used.

## Raw vs public pages

**Source-notes** may cite **`raw/processed/`** paths. **Published** wiki pages must remain **intelligible** without opening raw files: state the **claim**, the **authority type** (e.g. “NASS county table, 2024 survey”), and a **link** to the source-note or official reference.
