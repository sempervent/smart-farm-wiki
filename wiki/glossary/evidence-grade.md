# Evidence grade

**Evidence grade** describes **how strongly** a statement is backed for **decision use**: **primary / regulatory** (statute, agency manual, survey table), **extension / technical handbook**, **modeled / worksheet** (explicit assumptions), or **exploratory** (anecdote, forum, vendor marketing).

**Canonical page**: A wiki page that **owns** durable scope for a topic; **not** automatically “true,” but **the place to edit** when doctrine changes.

**Usage**: Prefer labeling **authority** in text (“NASS 2024 county estimate,” “TDEC rule set—verify with county”) over implying all analyses carry equal weight.

**Full pattern**: [`Evidence grade and canonical authority`](../concepts/evidence-grade-and-canonical-authority.md).
