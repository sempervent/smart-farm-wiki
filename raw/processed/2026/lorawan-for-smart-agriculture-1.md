## Data-Driven Sustainable Farming

The Internet of Things (IoT) creates remote access to sensors across a farm, enabling efficient, real-time monitoring of many factors that have traditionally been checked manually, on foot, or by look or feel.

LoRaWAN is an IoT radio technology most suitable for smart agriculture because it offers connectivity from a central location out as far as 15km. Sensors are wireless, making for easy installation and have a battery life of up to 10 years, which minimizes maintenance. One remarkable feature of LoRaWAN is that it works well in remote locations, where other connectivity solutions cannot.

With LoRaWAN, farmers can monitor and remotely manage everything from soil condition to irrigation to livestock health. The result of having access to this data is more efficient operational practices, savings of time and money, and improved yield.
