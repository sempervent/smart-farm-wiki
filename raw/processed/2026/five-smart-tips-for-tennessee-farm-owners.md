5 tips every Tennessee farm owner should know.

## Are You a Tennessee Farm Owner?

Owning a farm in Tennessee is both a rewarding lifestyle and a significant investment. Whether you run a small hobby farm or manage a large agricultural operation, staying informed and proactive is key to long-term success. Here are five tips every Tennessee farm owner should know:

**1\. Know Your Land’s Zoning and Tax Benefits**

Understanding how your land is zoned is essential when planning improvements, expansions, or changes in operation. Tennessee also offers Greenbelt tax incentives that can significantly reduce property taxes for agricultural, forest, or open-space land. Take time to ensure your land qualifies and that your paperwork is up to date with your local assessor.

**2\. Invest in Soil Health and Conservation**

Healthy soil is the foundation of a productive farm. Schedule regular soil testing to monitor nutrient levels and pH balance. Consider incorporating sustainable practices like cover cropping, no-till farming, and rotational grazing to maintain soil fertility and prevent erosion—especially in Tennessee’s rolling hills and varied terrain.

**3\. Stay Weather-Prepared**

Tennessee farmers deal with everything from spring floods to summer droughts and winter ice. Have an emergency plan in place for both your livestock and equipment. Investing in proper drainage, backup generators, and weather-resistant infrastructure can save you thousands in the long run.

**4\. Diversify Your Income Streams**

From agritourism and farm-to-table products to leasing hunting rights or adding solar panels, there are many ways to generate additional income from your land. Diversification not only increases revenue but also builds resilience in tough agricultural years.

**5\. Build a Strong Local Network**

Connect with local co-ops, extension offices, and neighboring farmers. These relationships can provide vital support, up-to-date resources, and potential business opportunities. Groups like the Tennessee Farm Bureau and UT Extension offer workshops, market access, and policy updates tailored to farm owners.

Owning a farm in Tennessee is more than a job—it's a way of life. With smart strategies and community support, you can make your land work for you for generations to come.
